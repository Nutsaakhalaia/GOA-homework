Ice hockey originated in Canada in the mid-19th century. While the exact origins are debated, the modern version of the sport is based on European field hockey and a variety of games played on ice in Canada, particularly in the province of Ontario.
The first recorded indoor ice hockey game took place in Montreal in 1875, where teams played with a flat rubber puck.
The National Hockey League (NHL) was founded in 1917 in Canada and originally had just 4 teams.
Ice hockey is often called “the fastest game on ice” due to its incredibly fast pace and intense physical play.
  • Helmets (to protect against head injuries)
  • Pads (shoulder, elbow, knee, and shin pads),
  • Gloves (for hand and finger protection),
  • Mouthguards,
  • Skates (designed specifically for quick, sharp turns and fast skating).

While ice hockey is often associated with Canada, the United States, and Europe, hockey has grown in countries like Russia, Sweden, Finland, and the Czech Republic.
Internationally, the IIHF (International Ice Hockey Federation) oversees global competition and organizes tournaments such as the World Championships and the Olympics.
